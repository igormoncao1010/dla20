IFC and Government of Belize Sign MoU to Scale Up Public-Private Partnerships

The International Finance Corporation (IFC) and the Government of Belize have signed a Memorandum of Understanding to develop Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) that will boost investments in energy, infrastructure, logistics, and digitalization. This agreement aims to strengthen the state's capacity to implement projects with private capital, driving economic growth and modernization.

ECLAC Projects Modest Economic Growth for Belize in 2025-2026

The Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (ECLAC) projects Belize's economy to grow only 1.5% in 2025 and about 2.5% in 2026, reflecting a regional scenario of sluggish growth due to lower global demand for tourism and services, high energy expenses, and vulnerability to natural disasters.

San Pedro Records Highest Inflation in Belize in July 2025

The tourist town of San Pedro recorded inflation of 3.1% in July 2025, above the national average, driven by price increases in food, rent, and services. This raises concerns about the cost of living despite falling fuel prices, highlighting economic pressures in popular tourist destinations.

Belize Gets WTO Funds for Fisheries Sustainability Project

The Government of Belize secured a $300,000 grant from the World Trade Organization's Fisheries Fund to legally and institutionally empower the country in managing fisheries subsidies. This aligns with sustainability initiatives and supports Belize's commitment to sustainable marine resource management.
